NVIDIA released Nemotron 3 Ultra, an open 550 billion parameter model with 55 billion active per token. It is a hybrid Mamba-Transformer mixture of experts, it holds up to a million tokens of context, and it is purpose-built for long-running agents that plan and call tools across many turns. The weights are open under a permissive license, so anyone can self-host it. On OpenRouter it is free through June 18 if you pick the :free variant.

The security story is the length. A long-running agent keeps a giant context across hundreds of tool-calling turns, and that context is a durable target. One planted instruction in a fetched page, a document, or a tool's output can sit quietly and steer the agent later. That is indirect prompt injection and context poisoning, stretched over a long session. Open weights also mean there is no vendor moderation, no rate limit, and no kill switch standing behind the model. And this is not theoretical. In its first week on OpenRouter's free tier it pulled tens of billions of tokens a day, peaking over 100 billion prompt tokens, and the most-used public app on it was an autonomous agent, Nous Research's Hermes.

So put the control where you actually own it, outside the model. Require human or policy approval for irreversible and high-impact tool calls. Log the agent's stated goal every turn so goal drift is something you can see. Treat the growing context window as untrusted input, not trusted memory.

Built-in guardrails are optional when the weights are open. The permission layer around your tools is the control that holds.
