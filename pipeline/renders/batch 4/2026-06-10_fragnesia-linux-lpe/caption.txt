Fragnesia (CVE-2026-46300) is the third Linux kernel LPE in two weeks using page-cache corruption. Targets XFRM ESP-in-TCP subsystem. Discovered by William Bowling of V12 Security.

Separate bug from Dirty Frag but same surface and same mitigation. Deterministic page-cache corruption. No race condition. Immediate root on all major distros by corrupting /usr/bin/su page cache.

Unlike Dirty Frag, no host-level privileges required. AppArmor namespace restrictions are only partial mitigation, and additional bypasses are needed.

The pattern: Copy Fail (algif_aead) -> Dirty Frag (xfrm-ESP + RxRPC) -> Fragnesia (xfrm ESP-in-TCP). Three distinct subsystems. Same page-cache corruption primitive. Same root via /usr/bin/su. Same mitigation: blocklist esp4, esp6, rxrpc.

CloudLinux: Customers with Dirty Frag mitigation need no further action until patched kernels. Red Hat assessing if mitigation extends. Microsoft urges same mitigations. No in-the-wild exploitation observed yet.
