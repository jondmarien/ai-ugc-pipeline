Dirty Frag is the successor to Copy Fail. It chains two page-cache write primitives in the Linux kernel: xfrm-ESP (IPSec subsystem) and RxRPC. Together they give deterministic local root on most distributions.

Unlike race-condition exploits, Dirty Frag is a deterministic logic bug. No timing window. Kernel does not panic on failure. Very high success rate. One command root.

The chain covers blind spots: xfrm-ESP needs user namespaces (blocked by Ubuntu AppArmor). RxRPC needs no namespace but rxrpc.ko is not in most distros — except Ubuntu loads it by default. One works where the other fails.

The Copy Fail mitigation (blacklisting algif_aead) does not stop Dirty Frag. It works regardless of that module. CloudLinux advises blocklisting esp4, esp6, and rxrpc. But that breaks IPsec/RxRPC functionality.

Microsoft observed limited in-the-wild exploitation: SSH access, then ELF binary triggers privilege escalation via su, modifies GLPI LDAP auth, accesses sensitive data, wipes PHP sessions.

CVE-2026-43284 (xfrm-ESP) and CVE-2026-43500 (RxRPC) patched in mainline. Ubuntu, Debian, RHEL, Fedora, SUSE, Amazon, AlmaLinux, Rocky, CloudLinux all have advisories.

Dirty Frag proves the bug class is wider than one subsystem. Patch the kernel, not the module.
