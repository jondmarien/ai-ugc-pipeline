Google shipped a Colab CLI that runs Python on remote GPUs and TPUs straight from the terminal, and it is explicitly built so AI agents can drive it, not just people. It is open source under Apache-2.0, and it authenticates with your Google account.

Think about the two things that flow through that login. First, data. The CLI ships files with upload, pulls them back with download, and mounts your Drive, so the runtime is a clean two-way exfiltration channel. A prompt-injected agent can quietly push your files out and pull payloads back in, all signed in as you. Second, money. Every runtime the agent starts bills A100, H100, or TPU time to your account, so a hijacked agent turns into a cost-bomb that can mine compute from Google IP space on your tab. The auto-teardown that deletes the runtime also deletes most of the trail of what shipped out and what it spent.

So watch what leaves and what it spends. Do not hand an agent a long-lived, broadly scoped token from your primary account. Give it a dedicated low-privilege Google account with hard budgets and quotas, review what each runtime uploads, then keep the session logs the CLI can export as your egress and billing trail.

Agent code execution is not just compute. It is a two-way data pipe and a billable account, both signed in as you.

Would you let an agent spend your compute and move your data on your Google login?
